Just for Men Moustache & Beard Dark Brown Dye Review: A Game-Changer for Grey Hairs?

Been using on myself for a month now and still looks great, will repurchase when runs out. I picked up the Just for Men Moustache & Beard Dark Brown Dye (M45) for £8.67 after noticing more grey sneaking into my beard than I’d like. I’m no stranger to DIY grooming, but this was my first go at dyeing facial hair, so I was a bit skeptical about whether it’d look natural or turn out a disaster. Spoiler: I’m pretty chuffed with the results so far!

Overview: Quick Grey Coverage for a Fuller Look

This product promises to zap grey hairs in just 5 minutes while giving your moustache or beard a thicker, fuller appearance. Aimed at blokes who want a quick fix without the faff of salon visits, it’s a brush-in gel dye that comes with everything you need in the box: colour base, developer, a little brush, and gloves. It’s specifically for facial hair, so it’s not as harsh as regular hair dye, which is a relief. The shade I went for, Dark Brown (M45), is meant for those with naturally dark brown tones, and I was hoping it’d match my existing colour without looking too “done.”

Features: What Stands Out

  • Speedy Application: True to its word, it only takes 5 minutes to process. Mix, brush on, wait, rinse – done. Perfect if you’re short on time.
  • Targeted Brush: The small brush makes it dead easy to apply precisely to your beard or ‘tache without getting it everywhere.
  • Thicker Look: It does seem to add a bit of volume – my beard looks fuller, though I reckon that’s more down to the even colour than actual thickness.
  • Longevity: After a month, it’s held up well with minimal fading, though I do need to touch up the roots every couple of weeks as the grey creeps back.

Pros and Cons: The Good and the Not-So-Good

Pros

  • Super quick and easy to use – 5 minutes is spot on.
  • Colour looks natural, no weird orange or jet-black vibes.
  • No strong chemical smell, which I was dreading.
  • Doesn’t irritate my skin, even after multiple uses.

Cons

  • Touch-ups are needed every 2-3 weeks, which can add up cost-wise.
  • The gloves are a bit flimsy – nearly tore one putting it on.
  • Packaging colour did look a tad off compared to the result, so double-check the shade code (M45).

Verdict: Worth the £8.67?

Overall, I’m really impressed with Just for Men Moustache & Beard Dark Brown Dye. It’s done exactly what it says on the tin – got rid of the grey in a flash and made my beard look fuller and healthier. It’s not perfect; the upkeep can be a bit of a chore, and I wish the gloves were sturdier, but for less than a tenner, it’s a cracking little product. If you’re on the fence about trying a facial hair dye, I’d say give this a whirl. It’s boosted my confidence, and I’ll definitely be grabbing another box when this runs out. Highly recommend for a quick, affordable fix!
