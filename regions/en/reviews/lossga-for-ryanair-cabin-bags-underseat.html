Lossga for Ryanair Cabin Bags Underseat 40x30x20 Anti Theft Carry-ons Review

Got this to save me extra cash on carry-on fees - works so far. I’ve been on the hunt for a decent underseat bag that fits Ryanair’s strict size limits without costing a fortune, and at £15.99, the Lossga Cabin Bag seemed like a bit of a steal. I’ve taken it on a couple of short trips already, so here’s my honest take after putting it through its paces.

Overview of the Lossga Cabin Bag

The Lossga Cabin Bag is marketed as the perfect fit for Ryanair’s underseat dimensions of 40x30x20cm, though the product description notes it’s actually closer to 40x20x28cm. There’s a bit of wiggle room with the elastic material, which they claim helps squeeze it under the seat to avoid those pesky extra fees. It’s also compatible with other budget airlines like EasyJet and Wizz Air, which is handy if you’re a frequent flyer on different carriers. At just £15.99, it’s clearly aimed at budget-conscious travellers who want something functional without splashing out.

Features and Performance

First off, the size is pretty much spot on for Ryanair’s rules, though I did notice the 2-3cm variation they mention in the description depending on how much I stuffed in. The elastic material does help compress it a bit, which is great for sliding it under the seat, but I wouldn’t say it feels super sturdy because of that. It’s got an anti-theft design with hidden zips, which gave me some peace of mind in busy airports, though I’m not sure how effective it’d be against a determined thief.

There’s a water bottle holder on the side, which is a nice touch for keeping my drink handy without taking up space inside. The main compartment is decent for a weekend’s worth of clothes, and there are a couple of smaller pockets for bits like my passport and charger. I wouldn’t say it’s packed with fancy features, but for the price, it does the job.

The Lossga Cabin Bag in action - fits nicely under the seat!

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Great price at £15.99 - proper budget-friendly.
  • Fits Ryanair’s underseat rules (most of the time).
  • Water bottle holder is a handy addition.
  • Anti-theft zips add a bit of security.

Cons

  • Elastic material feels a tad flimsy - not sure about long-term durability.
  • Size can vary by a couple of cm depending on how full it is.
  • Not the most spacious if you’re a heavy packer.

Verdict

Overall, the Lossga Cabin Bag is a decent buy for £15.99 if you’re after something cheap and cheerful to dodge Ryanair’s carry-on fees. It’s not the most robust bag I’ve ever owned, and I’ve got my doubts about how it’ll hold up after a few more trips, but for now, it’s done the job on short flights. The water bottle holder and anti-theft zips are nice little bonuses, though I wouldn’t rely on it for anything too valuable. If you’re a light packer and just need something for weekend getaways, it’s worth a punt. I’d give it a solid 3.5 out of 5 - good value, but don’t expect miracles.
