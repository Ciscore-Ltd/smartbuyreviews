The Housemaid: An absolutely addictive psychological thriller with a jaw-dropping twist

Surprised how much I liked it despite initial doubts about psychological thrillers.

Overview

So, I picked up 'The Housemaid' for a mere £3, thinking it'd be just another run-of-the-mill thriller. But boy, was I wrong! From the get-go, the story had me hooked with its intriguing plot and unpredictable twists. It's the kind of book that you start reading and before you know it, you're halfway through.

Features

The book is a compact paperback, perfect for slipping into your bag for a read on the go. The narrative is fast-paced, with short chapters that keep you turning the pages. The twist at the end? Let's just say it's one of those moments that makes you go, 'Wow, didn't see that coming!'

Pros and Cons

Pros:

  • Engaging from start to finish
  • Unexpected twist that's genuinely surprising
  • Affordable price at just £3

Cons:

  • Some characters could have been developed more
  • The plot can feel a bit rushed at times

Verdict

All in all, 'The Housemaid' is a cracking read for anyone who loves a good psychological thriller. It's got all the elements that make for a gripping story, and that twist at the end is just the cherry on top. If you're looking for a quick, thrilling read that won't break the bank, this is definitely worth picking up.
