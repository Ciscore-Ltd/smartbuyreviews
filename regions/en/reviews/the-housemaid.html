Overview

I grabbed this Kindle book for a tenner—wait, three quid actually—on a rainy commute home from work in Manchester, and bloody hell, it hooked me straight away. It's this psychological thriller about a down-on-her-luck housemaid who takes a job with a posh family in their massive gated house. Things start off weird and just get darker and more twisted. Freida McFadden knows how to build that creeping dread, and I devoured it over two evenings, ignoring the footie on telly.

The story's told in first person, which makes you feel like you're right there in Millie's head, piecing together the madness. At under 350 pages, it's a quick read but packs a punch—perfect for when you want something that'll stick with you.
