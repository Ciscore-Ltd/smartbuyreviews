Just for Men Moustache & Beard Dark Brown Dye Review: Does It Really Eliminate Grey?

Got this to cover grey hairs on my sideburns, works well for that. I’ve been noticing more and more grey sneaking into my beard and moustache lately, and at 42, I’m not quite ready to embrace the silver fox look just yet. So, I picked up the Just for Men Moustache & Beard Dark Brown Dye (M45) for £8.67 on Amazon, hoping it’d give me a quick fix and a bit of confidence back. Here’s my honest take after using it for a couple of weeks.

Overview: What’s This Dye All About?

This product promises to zap grey hairs in just 5 minutes while making your facial hair look thicker and fuller. It’s specifically designed for beards and moustaches, which is great because I’ve tried regular hair dye before and it’s just not the same. The dark brown shade (M45) seemed like a safe bet for my natural colour, and the packaging, while a bit basic, gets straight to the point with clear instructions. It’s a gel formula that you mix yourself, and it comes with a little brush for easy application.

Features: What Stands Out?

  • Quick Application: True to the claim, it only takes 5 minutes to set. Mix the colour base and developer, slap it on with the brush, wait, and rinse. Dead easy.
  • Targeted Design: The small brush is ace for getting into tricky spots like sideburns or a patchy beard without staining your skin too much.
  • Shade Matching: The dark brown isn’t too harsh or fake-looking. It blended decently with my natural colour, though it’s not an exact match.
  • Thickening Effect: It does give a slightly fuller appearance, probably because the dye coats the hairs a bit, but don’t expect miracles if your beard is sparse.

Pros & Cons: The Good and the Not-So-Good

Pros

  • Effectively covers grey hairs, especially on sideburns and thicker patches.
  • Super quick to use – perfect if you’re in a rush before a night out.
  • Doesn’t feel harsh on the skin, no major irritation after using it.
  • Good value at £8.67 for a few applications if you’re careful with portions.

Cons

  • Colour fades a bit after a week or so, needs reapplying sooner than expected.
  • Can be a faff to mix and apply without getting it on your hands or sink.
  • Not as dark as I’d hoped – needed a second go for some stubborn greys.
  • Smell isn’t great, bit chemical, though it doesn’t linger after rinsing.

Verdict: Worth the £8.67?

Overall, I’m pretty chuffed with the Just for Men Moustache & Beard Dark Brown Dye. It’s not perfect – the colour could last a tad longer, and the mixing is a bit of a chore – but it does the job of covering grey and giving my facial hair a bit of oomph. For under a tenner, it’s a decent shout if you’re after a quick fix rather than a permanent solution. I’ll probably keep using it every couple of weeks to maintain the look, especially for those sneaky sideburn greys. If you’re on the fence, I’d say give it a go, just manage your expectations on the ‘thicker and fuller’ claim.
