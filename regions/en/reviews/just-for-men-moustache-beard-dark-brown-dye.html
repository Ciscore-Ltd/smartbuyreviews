Just for Men Moustache & Beard Dark Brown Dye Review: A Quick Fix for Grey?

Decent enough for a quick fix, but not a miracle worker. That’s my first impression of the Just for Men Moustache & Beard Dark Brown Dye (M45). I picked this up for £8.67 on Amazon, hoping to zap the grey in my beard and moustache and get that thicker, fuller look it promises. I’ve tested it out over a couple of applications now, so here’s my honest take on whether it’s worth your hard-earned cash.

Overview: What’s This Dye All About?

This product from Just for Men is marketed as a fast and easy solution to banish grey hairs in your facial fuzz. It claims to take just 5 minutes to work, targeting only the grey bits for a natural-looking result. The shade I went for is Dark Brown (M45), and it’s meant to blend in with your existing colour while making your beard and moustache appear thicker and fuller. The box warns that packaging colour may vary, but the contents are the same. It’s a beauty product, sure, but it’s more about confidence than vanity for me.

Features: How Does It Hold Up?

First off, the application process is pretty straightforward. You mix the colour base and developer in the little tray provided, slap it on with the brush, and wait 5 minutes before rinsing. It’s quick, I’ll give it that. The dye is designed to penetrate coarse facial hair, which is a plus since beard hair can be stubborn. It also comes with enough for multiple touch-ups if you’re just doing small areas. The smell isn’t too bad either – a bit chemical, but nothing that’ll make your eyes water.

One thing I noticed is that it doesn’t feel harsh on the skin, at least not for me. There’s no major irritation, though I did patch test first as recommended. The colour result? It’s alright. The grey is mostly gone after one go, and it does look a bit fuller, but don’t expect a complete transformation. It’s subtle, which might be good or bad depending on what you’re after.

Pros and Cons: The Good and the Not-So-Good

Pros

  • Quick 5-minute application – perfect if you’re in a rush.
  • Decently covers grey hairs for a natural-ish look.
  • No major skin irritation for me, even on sensitive areas.
  • Good value at £8.67 for multiple uses.

Cons

  • Can be messy – stained my sink a bit during rinse-off.
  • Fuller look is subtle, not dramatic as advertised.
  • Colour fades a bit after a week or so, needs reapplication.
  • Brush is fiddly for precise areas like sideburns.

Verdict: Should You Buy It?

Overall, Just for Men Moustache & Beard Dark Brown Dye does the job if you’re after a quick and easy way to hide the grey. For £8.67, it’s not a bad shout, especially since you get a few uses out of one box. It’s not going to turn back the clock or give you a beard like a Viking, but it’s a decent pick-me-up for a more polished look. I’d use it again for a special occasion or if I’ve got a Zoom call where I don’t want to look knackered. If you’ve got stubborn greys and don’t mind a bit of faff with the application, it’s worth a try.
